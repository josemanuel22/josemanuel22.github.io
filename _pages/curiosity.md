## Data Representation, uncertain data and queries

Some time ago I found this great [survey](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2003.12590.pdf) that summarizes the state of the art of data representation. Regarding this survey, I was able to find two other very interesting related works, [Stable Tuple Embeddings for Dynamic Databases](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2103.06766.pdf), [Creating Embeddings of Heterogeneous Relational Datasets for Data Integration Tasks](https://dl.acm.org/doi/pdf/10.1145/3318464.3389742). I hope one day to have time to add a blog entry summarizing these three works ;)

## Distributed systems

Regarding distributed systems, I recommend the classic [bitcoin white paper](https://bitcoin.org/bitcoin.pdf). It is beautiful to discover how economics, technology, mathematics and even philosophy can be combined in a couple of pages.

I have also read this classic paper, [Viewstamped Replication Revisited](https://pmg.csail.mit.edu/papers/vr-revisited.pdf). It is always uselful to remember how difficult the world is when we live in a no-ideal, distributed world where things can go very wrong.

Finally, [Martin Kleppmann's YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/@kleppmann) seems to me a great entry point to enter this world. If you like his channel, don't forget to take a look at his book, [Designing Data-Intensive Applications](https://amzn.to/3HmB69R).

## Artificial intelligence 

I have been able to read quite a few papers on artificial intelligence. Of all the artificial intelligence ideas, the one that attracts me the most is the graph neural network (GNN), the following surveys summarize the state of the art very well, [A comprehensive survey on graph neural network](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1901.00596.pdf), [HOW POWERFUL ARE GRAPH NEURAL NETWORKS?](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1810.00826.pdf).
